##  Introduction

Phishing is one of the most common cyber attacks used to steal:

- Login credentials  
- OTPs  
- Personal and sensitive information  

Attackers usually target **people, not systems**.

This report analyzes a phishing email and provides **simple awareness guidelines** that any employee can easily understand and follow.

---

##  Objective of This Report

The main goals of this report are to:

- Analyze phishing email samples  
- Identify common phishing indicators (**red flags**)  
- Classify email risk clearly  
- Explain how phishing attacks work (simple explanation)  
- Provide clear **Do’s and Don’ts** for employees

##  Sample Phishing Email Analyzed

###  Email Details

- **Subject:**  Urgent: Your Account Will Be Locked  
- **Sender:** `security@secure-account-verify.com` *(Fake)*  

###  Email Body

> Dear User,  
>  
> We noticed suspicious activity on your account.  
> To avoid account suspension, please verify your details immediately.  
>  
>  **Verify Now:** `http://secure-account-verify[.]com`  
>  
> Failure to verify within 24 hours will result in permanent account lock.  
>  
> Regards,  
> Security Team

---

##  Phishing Indicators Identified

| **Indicator** | **Observation** | **Why It Is Dangerous** |
|--------------|---------------|-------------------------|
| Fake Sender Domain | `secure-account-verify.com` | Not an official company domain |
| Urgency | “Account will be locked in 24 hours” | Forces panic decisions |
| Suspicious Link | HTTP (not HTTPS), unknown domain | Can steal login details |
| Generic Greeting | “Dear User” | Legit companies use real names |
| Fear-Based Language | Threat of permanent lock | Common phishing tactic |

---

##  Email Risk Classification

**Risk Level:**  **PHISHING**

###  Reasons

- Fake sender domain  
- Urgent and threatening language  
- Suspicious link  
- No user personalization  

**Action:**  
This email should be **deleted immediately** and **reported to the security team**.

---

##  How This Phishing Attack Works (Simple Explanation)

1. User receives an urgent email  
2. Email creates fear (account suspension)  
3. User clicks the fake link  
4. Fake website asks for login details  
5. Attacker steals credentials  
6. User account gets compromised  

---

##  Prevention Guidelines for Employees

###  Do’s

- Check the sender’s email address carefully  
- Hover over links before clicking  
- Verify urgent emails using official websites  
- Report suspicious emails to IT/Security team  

###  Don’ts

- Do not click unknown or suspicious links  
- Do not download attachments from unknown senders  
- Do not share passwords or OTPs  
- Do not panic because of urgent messages  

---

##  Employee Awareness Checklist

Ask yourself the following questions:

-  Is the sender domain correct?  
-  Is the email creating urgency or fear?  
-  Does the link look suspicious?  
-  Is my name missing?  

 **If ANY answer is YES, treat the email as phishing.**

---

## Conclusion

Phishing attacks succeed mainly due to **lack of awareness**.

By learning how to identify phishing indicators and following simple safety practices, employees can:

- Prevent credential theft  
- Avoid account compromise  
- Reduce major security incidents
